About Prabhakar

Revolutionizing Environmental Exploration

Prabhakar is a cutting-edge solar-powered rover designed to autonomously explore and monitor remote environments while collecting valuable scientific data. Named after the Bengali word for "sun-powered," this innovative vehicle represents a significant advancement in sustainable exploration technology.

Equipped with state-of-the-art sensors, high-resolution cameras, and advanced solar tracking systems, Prabhakar can operate for extended periods in challenging environments without human intervention. Its primary mission is to document environmental conditions, analyze soil properties, and capture multimedia content of remote locations that are difficult for humans to access.

The rover's design emphasizes sustainability, utilizing renewable solar energy as its primary power source. This approach minimizes environmental impact while maximizing operational duration in field conditions.

Advanced Solar Power System

Prabhakar utilizes high-efficiency monocrystalline solar panels with an advanced tracking system that follows the sun's path throughout the day, maximizing energy capture by up to 40% compared to fixed panels.

  • Triple-junction solar cells with 28% efficiency
  • Dual-axis solar tracking system
  • Intelligent power management with battery optimization
  • Backup battery system for continuous operation

Autonomous Navigation System

Prabhakar employs a sophisticated navigation system combining GPS, inertial measurement units (IMU), and computer vision to autonomously traverse challenging terrain while avoiding obstacles.

  • Dual-frequency GPS for centimeter-level accuracy
  • Stereo vision cameras for 3D environment mapping
  • LIDAR for obstacle detection and avoidance
  • AI-powered path planning algorithms

Comprehensive Sensor Array

The rover is equipped with a wide array of sensors to monitor environmental conditions, soil properties, and atmospheric parameters in real-time.

  • Multi-spectral soil analysis sensors
  • Atmospheric temperature, pressure, and humidity sensors
  • Soil moisture and composition sensors
  • Air quality monitoring sensors

Robust Communication System

Prabhakar features a multi-modal communication system that ensures reliable data transmission from remote locations back to the base station.

  • Long-range radio communication (up to 10km)
  • Satellite communication backup system
  • Real-time video streaming capability
  • Mesh networking for multi-rover operations

Our Institute

Advanced Robotics Research Institute

The Advanced Robotics Research Institute (ARRI) is a premier research facility dedicated to advancing robotics and autonomous systems. With state-of-the-art laboratories and a multidisciplinary team of researchers, ARRI focuses on developing innovative solutions for environmental monitoring, disaster response, and exploration.

Our mission is to push the boundaries of robotic technology while ensuring sustainability and environmental responsibility. The Prabhakar project represents our commitment to creating autonomous systems that can operate in challenging environments while minimizing ecological impact.

University Affiliation

National Institute of Technology, Kurukshetra

The National Institute of Technology, Kurukshetra (NIT KKR) is one of India's premier technical universities, known for its excellence in engineering education and research. Established in 1963, the institute has been at the forefront of technological innovation and has produced numerous leaders in various fields of engineering and technology.

The Prabhakar project is a collaborative effort between the Advanced Robotics Research Institute and the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at NIT Kurukshetra. This partnership combines cutting-edge research with academic excellence to advance the field of autonomous robotics.
